The Essentials

What is nature risk analysis?

Nature risk analysis helps companies understand how their operations depend on and impact biodiversity and ecosystem services. Kuyua assesses these risks at a location level — identifying where your assets are exposed to nature-related threats, quantifying the financial value at stake, and showing where early action can protect long-term business value.

Why it matters

Why nature risk matters
for your business

More than half of global GDP depends on nature. The businesses that act first will be best positioned to thrive.

Regulatory compliance

TNFD, CSRD, and EU Taxonomy now require companies to assess and disclose nature-related risks and dependencies.

Value chain exposure

Supply chains depend on healthy ecosystems. Biodiversity loss disrupts raw materials, water access, and agricultural inputs.

Financial materiality

Nature loss translates into real asset value loss. Kuyua quantifies this in monetary terms so you can prioritize action.

Investor expectations

ESG frameworks and institutional investors increasingly require biodiversity disclosure and science-based target-setting.

Our Process

How Kuyua
analyzes nature
& biodiversity risks

A science-based methodology aligned with TNFD LEAP — from location data to actionable nature intelligence.

1
Map your footprint

Geolocate all operations and supply chain sites against biodiversity-sensitive areas and ecosystem boundaries.

2
Assess dependencies

Identify which ecosystem services — water, pollination, soil, climate regulation — your business relies on at each site.

3
Evaluate impacts

Quantify how your operations affect local biodiversity, habitats, and threatened species in surrounding areas.

4
Score & benchmark

Generate comparable risk scores per location, aligned with TNFD LEAP, GRI, and double materiality standards.

5
Report & act

Export disclosures ready for CSRD, TNFD, and EU Taxonomy — and translate insights into mitigation and restoration actions.
